Takeaways
There have been important shifts in energy source composition that can be summarized as follows.
  • Coal is no longer king and coal generation is rapidly falling.

  • Gas is the new dominant source of U.S. electricity.

  • Hydro growth is limited by diminished reputation due to ecological concerns regarding rivers7.

  • Nuclear has peaked due to rising capital costs of next-generation, safer plants8.

  • Wind and Solar are the new kids in the block.

These trends in energy sources have played a role, though they are not the only factors, behind the declining carbon intensity of the economy of U.S. States.
